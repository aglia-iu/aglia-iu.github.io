Solar System VR - p5.js

This was the final project for ART 309 - Digital Art and Code with Meg Mitchell. Throughout the course of this class we spent a lot of time integrating traditional art critique and program development. The purpose of this project was to develop a connection between the way that we explore the connection between us on earth and the mighty expanse of space through sounds, where there naturally is none in space.

For this final project, I wanted to use a web-based medium to make the VR space accessible. I also used a Google Cardboard headset to demonstrate my final project. I used Perlin Noise to develop an algorithm that automatically creates the rocky terrain of terrestrial plants (Mercury, Venus, Mars) and ice giants (Neptune, Uranus) while creating a spherical atmosphere for gaseous planets(Jupiter and Saturn). I also spent a lot of time researching p5.xr, which helps create the XR interface. The program uses raycasting in order to highlight and then enter a planet's atmoshphere.
